It all started when three men set out to bring back the lost name of the mythical, magical plant — Hemp. The idea of "OG" hadn't even struck their minds yet. It was a trip to Nepal that changed the way they saw it.

Fibre To Favourite

OUR PROCESS

Six stages turn a hemp stalk grown in the field into the bag on your shoulder — every step chosen for the lightest possible footprint.

1

Cultivation & Harvest

Hemp plants grow for 4–5 months before the stalks are cut close to the ground at maturity. Seeds and flowers are set aside for other uses.

2

Retting

Stalks break down through dew, water, chemical or enzymatic retting — releasing the natural pectins that bind the fibres together.

3

Decortication

Dried stalks are crushed and beaten so rollers and breakers can separate the woody core from the usable bast fibre.

4

Crafting

Skilled hands weave the fibre into durable fabric, then cut and stitch it into the shape of your next favourite bag.

5

Packaging

Every bag is packed with care, so it reaches your doorstep exactly as it left the workshop.

6

Delivering

Your journey with us begins the moment your new hemp bag arrives — ready to be your everyday companion.
